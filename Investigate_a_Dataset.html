Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

The dataset consists of 100,000 medical appointments in Brazil, with a primary focus on determining whether patients attend their scheduled appointments or not. It also contains various characteristics related to the patients who have made these appointments. Our goal is to analyze this dataset in order to answer specific questions and identify factors that may influence attendance at these appointments. By examining the data, we aim to gain insights into the patterns and correlations that can help us better understand patient behavior and appointment attendance.

The dataset consists of several columns, each representing different variables or attributes.

PatientId ,AppointmentID, Gender ,ScheduledDay, AppointmentDay ,Age ,Neighbourhood,Scholarship ,Hipertension,Diabetes ,Alcoholism ,Handcap ,SMS_received ,No-show

1-Is there a correlation between gender and no-shows for appointments?

2-Which age group has a higher number of no-shows for appointments?

3-Is there a correlation between having a handicap and not showing up for appointments?

4- Does receiving SMS increase the chances of show-up?

Conclusions¶

In conclusion, the analysis revealed the following findings:1) There is no significant correlation between gender and appointment no-shows, indicating that gender does not significantly impact attendance. 2) Age, however, emerged as a significant factor, with young children aged 0-5 exhibiting higher attendance rates compared to other age groups. 3) There is no significant correlation between having a handicap and appointment non-attendance. 4) Surprisingly, individuals who did not receive SMS reminders had higher attendance rates than those who did, suggesting that SMS reminders may be ineffective in improving attendance. Future research should focus on investigating additional variables influencing non-attendance by gender, exploring factors contributing to higher attendance rates among young children, examining specific factors related to handicaps that impact attendance, and exploring alternative methods or communication channels to enhance attendance rates. Addressing these research gaps will provide valuable insights and inform strategies for improving appointment attendance in healthcare settings.
